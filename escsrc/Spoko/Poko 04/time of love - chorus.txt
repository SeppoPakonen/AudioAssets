Pronouns, comedy topic, humor explanation and abstract :
"love is not a work of art"
- pronouns: We
- comedy topic: Irony
- humor explanation: By giving the common saying “love is a work of art” a twist, the speaker reveals the fact that love isn't as easily crafted as a piece of art, thus creating a humorous irony. 
- abstract: A cheeky comment at the uncompromising culmination of love. 

"we crafted our love, not art."
- pronouns: We
- comedy topic: Contrasting and Unexpected Elements
- humor explanation: by juxtaposing the idea of creating art with the idea of feelings in a relationship, the speaker creates a humorous contrast to showcase how 'unartistic' relationships can be.
- abstract: A whimsical observation about relationship complexities, and the surprises that come with them.

"we loved until love fell apart."
- pronouns: We
- comedy topic: Subversion of Expectations
- humor explanation: By implying that even the strongest of relationships will eventually come to an end goes against the traditional view of everlasting relationships, creating a humorous irony.
- abstract: A lighthearted reminder that even relationships that seemed to be built to last are only temporary.

"it was a time of love, that I won't soon forget."
- pronouns: We
- comedy topic: Ironical Statements
- humor explanation: By emphasizing the relationship being a “time of love” in a morose tone implies a contradiction, suggesting that it wasn’t a positive experience despite being a time of love. 
- abstract: A muted yet sincere acknowledgment of the feelings felt during a special part of life, now past.

"not even in my dreams do I miss the time together."
- pronouns: We
- comedy topic: Surreal Humor
- humor explanation: By referencing “dreams”, the speaker surreally suggests that there aren’t even nostalgic feelings, even in his dreams, further emphasizing the lack of “miss” one would have of a significant other. 
- abstract: A whimsical imagining of a relationship gone but in which feelings are still far away.
