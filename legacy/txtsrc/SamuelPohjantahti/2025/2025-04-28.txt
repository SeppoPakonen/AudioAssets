Rules:
The New Workers' Song is a composition and lyrics competition created in collaboration with the Workers' Music Event and the Riihi Foundation, which seeks new workers' songs that respect tradition.
The competition seeks both traditional and modern approaches to composing workers' songs.
Peace songs have always been a part of the labor movement and workers' song tradition.
Traditionally, the songs deal with the theme of peace, reconciliation and a message against violence.
The message of these songs has been emphasized by the turbulent world situations.
Conflicts around the world are straining the general atmosphere and news headlines are causing anxiety.
On social media, others are being criticized anonymously and hostility is being incited. Now we need actions for peace!
What is today's peace song like and how does peace feel in everyday life and in the surrounding society?
The songs can be in different genres.
They share the common goal of inspiring the listener for peace.
A professional jury will evaluate each song.




Inspiration text:
Verse 1
Step into the hallway where the ceiling lights flicker.
Picture the nation stretched on a narrow bed.
Hear the monitor pulse like a distant siren.
Smell late-night coffee mixing with antiseptic.
See that the patient is everyone you know.
Realize that the chart lists your own name too.
The breath it takes next depends on invisible hands.
Let one of those hands belong to you.

Verse 2
Follow the corridor into the elder ward.
Parents wait behind curtains that muffle their sighs.
Ask how softly you closed the door last visit.
Ask how loudly you expect lawmakers to care.
Neglect, like infection, spreads by touch and silence.
Kindness, like medicine, multiplies the same way.
Budgets are stories scribbled in pencil.
Conscience is the pen that inks them permanent.

Chorus
Who stands the watch when the caretakers walk away?
Who shields the pulse that keeps the common body alive?
If not you, the night will answer with echoes.
If not now, the morning may never arrive.
The beeping question repeats without mercy.
Step closer; let your answer be a touch, not a word.

Verse 3
Remember the marches where hope hammered like drums.
Streets rose and fell beneath your chanting feet.
You swore the world would change before the echo died.
Tonight you change linens instead, but the oath still lives.
A clean pillow is a silent manifesto.
A whispered “how are you?” becomes a rally cry.
Every small mercy signs another petition for peace.
Heroes and orderlies share the same backbone.

Verse 4
Imagine soldiers stacking rifles like folded crutches.
Imagine diplomats locking briefcases full of threats.
Imagine workers everywhere putting danger on pause.
The globe would halt, yes, but it would stop bleeding.
Sometimes peace is the shift nobody shows up for.
Violence starves when its servants refuse the meal.
A single unified “no” can outweigh a thousand commands.
Silence, when chosen together, can boom like thunder.

Bridge
You have thought of closing your eyes to spare your heart.
Protection is wisdom until it turns to stone.
True calm radiates outward like clean light.
Let your guarded mind open a gate for others.

Verse 5
Picture the State as one restless patient tearing at tubes.
It curses the hands that hold the bandage in place.
Walking away would be easy, even justified.
Staying could rewrite tomorrow’s chart.
The choice circles back to a single pulse in your wrist.
What you decide echoes down every corridor.
Even a whisper here can quake a capital there.
A steady hand tonight drafts the treaty of next year.

Final Chorus
Who stands the watch when the caretakers walk away?
Who keeps the pulse when the bravest feel tired?
Let it be you, however shaky your fingers.
Let it be us, because many pulses make one heart.
This moment, this room, this life— all waiting.
Breathe deep, step forward, begin the healing.
