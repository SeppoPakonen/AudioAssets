Pronouns, comedy topic, humor explanation and abstract:
"Have a nice trip.":
- pronouns: us, we, our, you, I
- topic: Irony, absurd concepts, self-deprecating humor
- humor explanation: The speaker is being ironic by wishing the listener a nice trip when the listener is actually about to take off and is about to go on a journey that could end up being not so pleasant. The speaker also acknowledges the past fighting and presents the relationship between the two as a switch that goes from on to off and back again, creating a humorous image of the situation. 
- abstract: A speaker wishes the listener a nice trip despite the past fighting that drove them away and the joke that acknowledges the switch between on and off in their relationship.

"Let's remember the fights, so we won't get back together.":
- pronouns: us, we, our
- topic: Irony, subversion of expectations, contrast between dialogue and reality
- humor explanation: In the context of the previous statement, this line creates an ironic contrast as the speaker is seemingly wishing the listener a pleasant journey even though they both acknowledge that the fights between them make reconciliation impossible.
- abstract: A speaker creates an ironic contrast between their statement to wish the listener well and the reality that fights between them won't let them get back together.

"A switch between us: on and off, won't stay on, even if you put it on again.":
- pronouns: us, we, our, you
- topic: Irony, self-deprecating humor, absurd concepts
- humor explanation: By comparing their relationship to a switch, the joke highlights how fragile and unpredictable their relationship is, making it into a humorous statement. 
- abstract: A speaker jokes about their relationship being like a switch that goes from on to off and won't turn on again, creating an absurd yet humorous image.

"Let's go opposite ways without looking back.":
- pronouns: us, we, our
- topic: Atheistic interaction, subversion of expectations
- humor explanation: This line creates a contrast between what the speaker wishes for and the reality, as often times people do not want to simply go in opposite directions and instead wish to maintain a connection with another person. 
- abstract: A humorous contrast is created between what the speaker wishes for and what is deemed to be the normal expectation, as the speaker wishes to go opposite ways without looking back.

"I want to be like a train on the tracks, for which another oncoming train is deadly.":
- pronouns: I
- topic: Exaggeration, surreal situations, subversion of expectations
- humor explanation: By exclaiming that they want to be like a train on tracks, whose oncoming train is deadly, the speaker is extreme in their exaggeration of the situation and presents it in a humorous scenario. 
- abstract: In an exaggerated and humorous way, the speaker suggests that their situation is so dire that they mustn't encounter another individual.
