Background/Notes

Date: 20.7.2025
Info: Kappaleen idea tuli isäni katon sammaleita pois harjatessa. Ärsytti kaikki. Kesäisessä helteessä tein vaan jotain ilman sen suurempaa inspiraatiota. Alkuperäinen kappale saneli aika paljon sävyä. Oli lupa tehä melko negatiivinen biisi. Alkuperäinen sävellys oli vain eka projekti, jonka tein Cubase 2 SX:llä, ja testasin kitarajuttuja, jotka olin oppinut kaverini isoveljeltä Mikko Aaltoselta.
Tyyli: A female vocalist sings in Finnish over a pop-rock arrangement, The song is in a minor key and has a moderate tempo, The instrumentation includes a clean electric guitar playing arpeggiated chords, a bass guitar playing a walking bass line, and drums providing a steady beat with a prominent snare, The vocals are delivered in a clear, slightly melancholic tone, with some spoken word sections, The production is clean with a balanced mix, emphasizing the vocals and the rhythmic section, There are no significant dynamic shifts throughout the song, The song structure is verse-chorus based with an instrumental break
Background:
This song was hard to find from my archives. There was a early hole in my series of 20 years of summer songs. I remembered, that I indeed had a summer song for every year since 2002, but I could not figure out, what the 2003 year song was. While I was figuring other unrelated timeline stuff (what computers I had back then), I remembered what I did in 2003 summer. I was visiting our capital city (Helsinki, Finland) with my friend and we stayed at my friend's big brother's house in Toukola (and I bragged about my new Athlon XP 1800+ PC for him). He had a electric guitar and he encouraged us to learn how to play. He also gave me a dvd full of music production software, including Cubase 3 SX, NI Battery, some synths (ReFX Nexus etc.). The guitar playing actually sticked with me since that. My parents had pushed me to play acoustic guitar years earlier, but that had been very negative experience, and it did not lead to anywhere. Also, I just loved the electric guitar as a beautiful machine. So yeah, that is the story of the original name "guitar summer", which was also the name of the mp3 file, which I finally found.
This song was made in a crude way, because I was learning Cubase 3 SX, which was HARD. No tutorials, teachers, manual. Nothing. Just good ol' *try everything*.
The mp3 song has some filter effect, and that was what my friend's big brother taught to me before we left Helsinki. So, this song is one of the first songs that I made with Cubase. I did not play guitar yet and the guitar samples are from eJay Hiphop 5. I had exported only single samples as songs from eJay. It was not easy to download samples back then.
To summarize: this song is originally about Gibson Les Paul 59 Lemonburst (replica?) in Toukola, Helsinki, in summer 2003, which I adored. Also, there were some 2 nice punk bitches, which me and my friend talked almost the whole train trip back home. They showed some new punk bands, like AFI. I was, of course, the lame one, which these punk ladies did not approach.
29.6.2021: I fixed some *music theory issues*, like non-fitting bassline. I also added pad track, guitar solos and vocal melody. I think I am "required" to make lyrics about those 2 punk bitches, rather than about the Gibson Les Paul :/
